# Government Purchase

A specific implementation of a patent buyout where a government pays the innovator a value G* to open-source a technology, but faces significant free-rider problems in international funding. A policy where a single government buys out a patent and open-sources the technology, which is compared to the CRLM and can be less efficient due to fiscal costs. A mechanism where a collective of governments purchases an innovation at a set prize, which is formally identical to the cash Lindahl schedule but faces significant implementation challenges.
