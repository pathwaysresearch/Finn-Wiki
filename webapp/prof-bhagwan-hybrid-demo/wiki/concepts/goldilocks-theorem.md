# Goldilocks Theorem

A principle stating that the optimal goal for a mission is set strictly above expected output to maximize the marginal probability of success, being neither too easy nor too hard. A theorem concerning the optimal level of difficulty for collective goals, adding a new dimension to Goal-Setting Theory by considering strategic interaction among agents.
