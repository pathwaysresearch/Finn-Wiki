# Gordon Growth Model

A model used to compute the present value of the price at the end of a high growth phase, specifically for the stable growth phase. It calculates the price ($P_n$) as the expected dividends per share in the next period ($E(DPS_{n+1})$) divided by the difference between the cost of equity and the stable growth rate ($g$). It is a component of the two-stage dividend discount model.
