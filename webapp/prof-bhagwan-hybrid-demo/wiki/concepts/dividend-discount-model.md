# Dividend Discount Model

A valuation model that calculates the value of equity as the present value of expected dividends, strictly adhering to dividends as the cash flows to equity. While broadly applicable, it can undervalue companies that retain cash or overvalue those funding dividends externally, and it faces challenges when firms use stock buybacks instead of dividends. Its limitations can be addressed by extended equity valuation models.
