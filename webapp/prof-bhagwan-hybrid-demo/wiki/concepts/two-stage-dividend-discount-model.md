# Two-Stage Dividend Discount Model

A valuation model that calculates the value of equity as the present value of expected dividends during a non-stable growth phase and the present value of the price at the end of this high growth phase. The price at the end of the high growth phase is typically computed using the Gordon Growth Model. This model can adapt to companies with low or negative growth rates before reverting to stable growth.
