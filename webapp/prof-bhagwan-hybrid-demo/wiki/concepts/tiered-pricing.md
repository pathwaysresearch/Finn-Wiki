# Tiered Pricing

A policy requiring an innovator to sell at marginal cost in poor countries while maintaining monopoly prices in rich countries, which is politically feasible but results in sub-optimal innovation effort. A boundary case of the Capped Royalty Licensing Mandate where the royalty for poor countries is set to zero, resulting in access but sub-optimal innovation effort. A pricing strategy where different income groups or countries are charged different prices for the same product.
