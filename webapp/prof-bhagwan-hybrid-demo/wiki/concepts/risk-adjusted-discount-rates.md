# Risk-adjusted Discount Rates

A common approach in DCF valuation where the discount rate is adjusted upwards to account for the risk of the expected cash flows. If risk premiums from risk and return models are used, this approach yields the same value as certainty equivalent models. It discounts negative cash flows at a higher rate, making their present value less negative as risk increases.
