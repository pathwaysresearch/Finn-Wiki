# Book Value of Capital

An accounting measure of capital that reflects historical accounting choices, including depreciation, inventory valuation, and acquisition treatment. It is often used as a proxy for capital invested in assets in place, but requires significant adjustments, especially for older firms, to accurately reflect current capital investment.
