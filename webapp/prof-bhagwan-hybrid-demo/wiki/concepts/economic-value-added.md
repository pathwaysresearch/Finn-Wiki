# Economic Value Added (EVA)

A widely used variant of excess return models that measures the surplus value created by an investment or portfolio. It is computed as the product of the 'excess return' made on an investment and the capital invested. EVA is a simple extension of the Net Present Value (NPV) rule, where the NPV of a project is the present value of its EVA over its life. A valuation variant that builds on excess returns, where value is created by generating excess returns on investments. Its computation requires accounting for distortions in return on capital, cost of capital, and capital invested, often involving numerous adjustments to operating income and book capital. Understating the cost of capital can lead to overstating EVA.
