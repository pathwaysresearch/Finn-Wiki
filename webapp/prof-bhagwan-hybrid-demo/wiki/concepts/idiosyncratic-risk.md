# Idiosyncratic Risk

Risk that is unique to a specific company, also called firm-specific risk, which can be averaged out to nothing by holding a well-diversified portfolio. Firm-specific, uncorrelated risk that is not compensated because it can be diversified away in a large portfolio, as explained by the law of large numbers.
