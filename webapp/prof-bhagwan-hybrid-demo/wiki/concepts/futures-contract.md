# Futures Contract

A contract to exchange something in the future that is functionally similar to a forward contract, with the primary difference relating to the issue of being marked-to-market. A standardized contract, exactly like a forward contract but with exceptions, created to solve the problems of illiquidity and counterparty risk. A financial agreement described as a sequence of forward contracts that are cancelled and re-established daily, featuring daily settlement (mark to market) and an intermediary to mitigate counterparty risk.
