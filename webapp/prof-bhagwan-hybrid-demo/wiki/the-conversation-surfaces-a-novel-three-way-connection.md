# Genetic Distance, Trust, and Home Bias

A novel three-way connection: (1) genetic distance as a deep proxy for the trust deficit that drives home bias, (2) the threshold behavior in Ashraf-Galor's diversity-productivity relationship as analogous to threshold effects in systemic risk models, and (3) the implication that if home bias reflects ancient social distance, the policy remedy is not to reduce genetic distance but to build trust-substituting financial infrastructure — exactly what initiatives like FAB and standardized cross-border financial identity systems attempt to do.

This reframes home bias from a portfolio optimization failure into a missing financial infrastructure problem, which connects directly to the financial inclusion architecture.
