# Paper Entrepreneurship Model

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

This paper by Bhagwan Chowdhry and Nirvana Mitra (March 2026, preliminary draft) develops a formal model of entrepreneurial venture creation in which founders hold systematically upward-biased beliefs (delusion parameter Δ > 1) about project returns. A project of scale X succeeds with probability π(e), increasing in effort, and yields KX, but the entrepreneur believes the payoff is ΔKX. The central result is that entrepreneurial delusion amplifies effort by exactly the factor Δ relative to the social optimum, generating deadweight loss, yet for large-scale projects this delusion functions as an effort-and-participation technology inducing founders to bear costs rational agents would avoid. For small projects, however, delusion becomes a trap generating wasteful effort on ventures too small to justify it.

## Key Claims

- Entrepreneurial delusion (Δ > 1) amplifies founder effort by exactly the factor Δ relative to the socially optimal (first-best) effort level
- Outside investors price equity rationally and break even by construction, even though founders are deluded
- For large-scale projects, delusion serves as a beneficial effort-and-participation technology; for small projects, it generates wasteful effort and a welfare trap
- First-best project viability requires scale X ≥ X* = 4δ / (α²K²); below this threshold, no project is worth undertaking even under optimal effort
- The model builds on Landier and Thesmar (2009) but differs by focusing on the interaction between delusion and scale and deriving social welfare implications
