## Abstract

This is a long-form video interview with Dr. Martin L. Leibowitz, author of the classic *Inside the Yield Book* and the 1995 inaugural inductee into the Fixed-Income Analysts Society, conducted by Steve Foerster as part of the "In Pursuit of the Perfect Portfolio" project (MIT Laboratory for Financial Engineering, 2017). Leibowitz recounts his career journey from a mathematics doctorate to becoming the house mathematician at Salomon Brothers, where he revolutionized bond analytics by exposing fundamental flaws in standard yield calculations related to reinvestment rate risk. The interview traces his intellectual contributions to fixed-income analysis and portfolio management, offering a biographical and historical perspective on the evolution of bond market practice.

## Key Claims

- Standard bond yield calculations used by traders were flawed, particularly regarding the vulnerability to reinvestment rate assumptions and "interest on interest"
- When Leibowitz first presented corrected bond math to senior traders at Salomon Brothers, he was met with incredulity because yield-based thinking was so deeply embedded in practice
- Salomon Brothers was the dominant bond house of its era, yet had no house mathematician before Leibowitz joined in 1969
- Leibowitz's early work began with a manuscript on the mathematics of bonds that revealed discrepancies between theoretical bond behavior and actual numerical results
- Leibowitz had the only computer on the Salomon trading floor capable of calculating yields from prices and vice versa, which became critical when yields rose above 6% and senior traders lacked the tools to cope
