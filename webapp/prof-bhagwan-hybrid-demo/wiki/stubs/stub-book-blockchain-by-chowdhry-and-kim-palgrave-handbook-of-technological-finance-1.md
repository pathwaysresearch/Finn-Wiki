# Book Blockchain By Chowdhry And Kim Palgrave Handbook Of Technological Finance (1)

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

This chapter by Bhagwan Chowdhry and Seoyoung Kim, published in the Palgrave Handbook of Technological Finance, provides "A Layman's Guide to Bitcoin and Blockchain." It offers an intuitive, ground-up explanation of how Bitcoin and blockchain technology work, starting from foundational concepts like number systems (binary, hexadecimal), cryptographic hash functions (SHA-256), and building toward the Nakamoto Consensus mechanism that secures the Bitcoin blockchain. The piece contextualizes the rapid growth of the cryptocurrency ecosystem—over 5,000 exchange-traded cryptocurrencies, thousands of Bitcoin ATMs, and tens of billions in daily trading volume—while explaining how Bitcoin was designed to enable trustless peer-to-peer electronic payments without intermediaries.

## Key Claims

- Bitcoin was designed to provide an electronic payment system based on cryptographic proof instead of trust, allowing any two willing parties to transact without a trusted third party (citing Nakamoto 2008)
- The cryptocurrency ecosystem has grown to over 5,000 distinct exchange-traded cryptocurrencies, with Bitcoin averaging $27.5 billion USD in daily trading volume as of January 2020
- The SHA-256 hash function is deterministic and produces one of 2^256 possible codes, making it practically impossible to reverse-engineer the original input from a given hash
- Two seemingly similar inputs to SHA-256 can yield completely different hash codes, a property that further secures the system against guesswork
- The possibility of creating "trust" without intermediaries like banks, regulatory bodies, or governments has broad implications beyond payments
