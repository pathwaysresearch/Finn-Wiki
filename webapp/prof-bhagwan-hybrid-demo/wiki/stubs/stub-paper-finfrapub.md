## Abstract

This paper by Bhagwan Chowdhry, published in *Finance Research Letters*, proposes an equity-like auction protocol for financing land acquisition for large infrastructure projects. The mechanism does not require knowledge of the reservation value of the land being acquired nor determination of post-development land values ex ante. The model demonstrates that surrounding land values are capitalized by infrastructure investment, and this value uplift can be captured through a bidding process where competing landowners offer developed land parcels to the government. The landowner with the smallest private valuation wins the bid by offering the largest surrounding land area and maximizing the value of land offered to the government.

## Key Claims

- Land acquisition for infrastructure projects can be financed using an equity-like protocol based on an auction mechanism
- The auction protocol does not require knowledge of the reservation value of the land acquired or determination of post-development values
- Infrastructure investment produces benefits that are immediately capitalized into surrounding land values (citing World Bank perspective)
- The landowner with the smallest private valuation (v₀) will develop the largest land area around the project and maximize the value of land offered to the government (Proposition 1)
- The total value created from developing the ring of land surrounding the project is strictly positive (Δ(v) > 0), as post-development prices exceed the sum of development cost and private valuation within the relevant radius
