## Abstract

This source is primarily the book *Fintech for Billions* by Bhagwan Chowdhry and Anas Ahmed, published by Penguin Random House India. It examines whether financial inclusion initiatives in India—such as the Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)—have genuinely reached people at the bottom of the income pyramid, using research-based evidence and human stories. The authors propose a "SHUb" rubric (Simple, Human, Ubiquitous) to evaluate financial inclusion schemes and explore how India's world-class digital finance infrastructure can be leveraged to serve rural, unbanked populations. The book offers practical ideas for fintech to achieve universal and effective financial inclusion, with endorsements from figures including Raghuram Rajan, Nandan Nilekani, and Krishnamurthy Subramanian.

## Key Claims

- India's financial inclusion schemes like PMJDY have failed to achieve real financial inclusion for people at the bottom of the income pyramid, contrary to popular narratives
- Financial inclusion solutions should be evaluated using the SHUb framework: Simple, Human, and Ubiquitous
- Voice interfaces are important for reaching underserved populations in India
- India's digital finance infrastructure ("plumbing") is world-class but its potential for serving rural and unbanked citizens remains unrealized
- Solutions for financial inclusion must be low-cost, scalable, and tailored to the realities of micro-, small- and medium-sized enterprises and rural households facing healthcare emergencies and high borrowing costs
