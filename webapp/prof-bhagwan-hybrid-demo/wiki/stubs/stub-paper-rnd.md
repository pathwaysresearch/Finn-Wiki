# Paper Rnd

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

This paper by Bhagwan Chowdhry and Deepa Mani (ISB, March 2026) derives a mechanism called the Capped Royalty Licensing Mandate (CRLM) that achieves first-best innovation incentives and near-universal access to patented technologies—particularly pharmaceuticals—without requiring public expenditure or international fiscal coordination. The authors argue that Kremer-style patent buyouts and voluntary prize funds are theoretically sound but practically infeasible due to chronic free-rider problems (confirmed by COVAX's inability to raise sufficient funds). The CRLM works through a capped per-unit royalty licensing mandate where innovators license generic production in poor-country markets, arriving at a "Lindahl royalty" that optimizes welfare, and it dominates government purchase whenever the shadow cost of public funds exceeds approximately 21 cents per dollar.

## Key Claims

- Voluntary prize funds for innovation are chronically under-provided by a factor of √N due to free-rider problems, as demonstrated by COVAX at scale
- A single government buying out a patent at monopoly profit achieves only sub-optimal patent effort (since monopoly surplus is less than the socially optimal prize) and imposes fiscal welfare costs
- The CRLM achieves first-best innovation and near-universal access without public expenditure by mandating capped royalty licensing for generic production in poor-country markets
- Government purchase dominates the CRLM only if the shadow cost of public funds λ is below approximately 1.21 (i.e., less than 21 cents per dollar); at realistic shadow costs the CRLM wins
- The CRLM requires only a bilateral royalty-cap agreement rather than supranational prize funds or international fiscal coordination, making it strictly more implementable along four feasibility asymmetries
- The Lindahl royalty exhibits "racing invariance" and the framework formalizes an "access inversion" as a testable corollary
- At calibrated values, the socially optimal prize G* ≈ $77B for a global pharmaceutical, far exceeding what international coordination has ever achieved (COVAX raised ~$10B)
