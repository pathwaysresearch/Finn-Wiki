# Paper Which Firms Innovate V3

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

This paper by Bhagwan Chowdhry, Deepa Mani, and Madan Pillutla (November 2025) provides firm-level microfoundations for Schumpeter's creative destruction by building a three-date model integrating three economic forces: market uncertainty (which creates option value), organization capital and learning-by-doing, and identity/organizational coherence costs. The model explains why some incumbent firms innovate while others rationally do not, determining the scale and timing of innovation bets with mathematical precision. It generates formally derived and empirically testable predictions, using cases like Netflix vs. Blockbuster to illustrate how both innovating and not innovating can be rational decisions given a firm's specific circumstances.

## Key Claims

- Innovation decisions by incumbent firms can be modeled through three forces: market uncertainty (σ²), organization capital from learning-by-doing (s), and identity-disruption costs that raise the effective hurdle rate for innovation
- High market uncertainty creates disproportionately valuable expansion options (formalized as H·C(x₀, σ₀), a call-option expression strictly increasing in σ₀), with downside bounded by an abandonment option
- Both Blockbuster's decision not to innovate and Netflix's decision to innovate were rational at the time they were made, driven by the same three economic forces
- Organization capital — knowledge, routines, customer relationships, and operational expertise — makes future production in existing activities cheaper, creating a real economic cost to redeployment toward innovation
- Organizational identity coherence imposes an "identity-disruption premium" analogous to Mokyr's (2017) finding that rigid social structures systematically under-produce innovation at the societal level

## Topics Covered

Schumpeterian creative destruction, real options theory of innovation, organization capital and learning-by-doing (Arrow 1962), exploration vs. exploitation tradeoffs (March 1991), firm-level innovation microfoundations, identity-disruption premium, Netflix vs. Blockbuster case study, Aghion and Howitt formalization, abandonment options, entrepreneurial failure and real options (McGrath 1999), institutional prerequisites for technological progress (Mokyr 2017), three-date formal model of innovation timing and scale
