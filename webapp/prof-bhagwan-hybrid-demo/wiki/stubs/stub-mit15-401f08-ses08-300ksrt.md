## Abstract

This is a transcript of Session 8 from MIT's 15.401 Finance Theory course (Fall 2008), taught by Professor Andrew Lo. The lecture provides an overview of the equity business, including what equity ownership means, how primary and secondary markets function, and introduces models for pricing equities using previously developed financial tools. The lecture also covers growth opportunities, growth stocks, the role of limited liability in entrepreneurship, and the importance of short sales for market efficiency.

## Key Claims

- Equity holders are residual claimants who receive everything after bondholders are paid, giving them access to all upside of a company's growth and success
- Bondholders have first dibs on company assets but their claim is limited to the face value and coupons of the debt
- Limited liability is a powerful feature of modern capitalism that enables serial entrepreneurship, as illustrated by the Domino's Pizza founder who went through eight bankruptcies before his ninth company succeeded
- Short sales are important because they allow negative information to be incorporated into market prices, improving price efficiency
- IPO markets go through cycles tied to the business cycle and credit cycle, with periods of high activity followed by quieter periods
