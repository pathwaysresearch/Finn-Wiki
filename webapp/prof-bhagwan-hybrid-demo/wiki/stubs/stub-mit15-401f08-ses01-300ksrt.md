## Abstract

This is the transcript of the first lecture (Session 1) of MIT 15.401 Finance Theory, taught by Professor Andrew W. Lo at the MIT Sloan School of Management in Fall 2008. The lecture serves as a course introduction and motivational overview for first-year MBA students, covering why finance matters, the foundational role of time and risk in finance, and the six basic principles of finance. Lo introduces key individuals who personify the discipline and sets expectations for course requirements and mechanics over the 13-week semester.

## Key Claims

- Finance is the basic lingua franca of all business, making it one of the most important subjects for MBA students regardless of career path
- Without the two factors of time and risk, finance as a discipline would have no remaining open research questions or challenges
- The entire existence of finance departments, faculty, journals, and the finance industry is due solely to the presence of time and risk
- The course presents six basic principles of finance that students will encounter throughout all their finance courses at MIT and beyond
- Finance applies financial logic to help think smarter about all management decisions, not just finance-specific careers
