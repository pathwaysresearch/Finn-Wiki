## Abstract

This is a transcript of Session 3 of MIT 15.401 (Finance Theory I), Fall 2008, taught by Professor Andrew Lo. The lecture continues discussion of net present value (NPV), including a nuanced exploration of whether NPV can differ across currencies depending on exchange rate assumptions. The session also addresses real-time financial events, specifically the Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac bailout and its implications for the broader stock market, moral hazard, and systemic risk. Students are directed to the Brealey, Myers, and Allen textbook for supplementary terminology and material.

## Key Claims

- Net present value of a project could potentially differ in sign across currencies if the exchange rate is not constant over time, even in the absence of uncertainty
- If the exchange rate is the same at all time periods, it factors out as a positive constant and does not change the sign of NPV
- The distinction between "no uncertainty" and "fixed/constant" exchange rates is a critical subtlety in NPV analysis across currencies
- The stock market rally following the Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac bailout reflected not just direct exposure to their paper but broader systemic confidence effects across firms that did not hold their IOUs
- Government bailouts raise a fundamental tradeoff between the cost of moral hazard and the risk of allowing systemic financial contagion to spread, analogized to fighting wildfires
