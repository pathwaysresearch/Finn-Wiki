## Abstract

This is a long-form video interview with Charles D. ("Charlie") Ellis, best-selling author of *Winning the Loser's Game*, conducted by Steve Foerster as part of the MIT Laboratory for Financial Engineering project *In Pursuit of the Perfect Portfolio* (accompanying a forthcoming book by Stephen Foerster and Andrew W. Lo). The conversation traces Ellis's life and career trajectory—from his education at Harvard Business School, through working at the Rockefeller family office, to founding Greenwich Associates—while exploring his views on investing and portfolio construction. It provides biographical context for understanding how one of the most influential voices in index investing developed his philosophy.

## Key Claims

- Harvard Business School is described by Ellis as a genuinely "transformational experience," a characterization he says virtually every graduate would affirm
- Ellis entered the Rockefeller family office through a personal connection, initially thinking it was the Rockefeller Foundation (a non-profit), only to discover mid-interview it was the family's private investment office
- While at the Rockefeller office, Ellis was sent to a Wall Street firm's training program and took courses at NYU because the office recognized he needed investment knowledge he had not acquired at business school
- Ellis pursued a PhD over 14 years while simultaneously building his career and raising a family, motivated in part by his young son's remark that "you can't stop school till you finish"
- Ellis is positioned in the project as one of several key thinkers whose ideas contribute to understanding what constitutes a "perfect portfolio"
