# Paper Investing For Impact

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

This paper by Bhagwan Chowdhry (UCLA), Shaun William Davies (CU Boulder), and Brian Waters (CU Boulder) studies joint financing between profit-motivated and socially motivated (impact) investors, deriving conditions under which impact investments improve social outcomes. The core argument is that when project owners cannot commit to social objectives, impact investors should hold financial claims to counterbalance owners' tendencies to overemphasize profits, effectively subsidizing the direction of scarce resources toward social benefit production. The paper provides guidance on the design of contingent social contracts such as social impact bonds and pay-for-social-success contracts, and shows that pure nonprofit status may be optimal for the highest valued social projects.

## Key Claims

- Joint financing between for-profit owners and impact investors is mutually beneficial: owners receive a subsidy while impact investors enjoy increased social output
- When for-profit owners cannot commit to social objectives, impact investors should hold financial claims to counterbalance the owner's profit emphasis
- Impact investors' ownership stakes should be higher when the value of social output is higher
- Pure nonprofit status may be optimal for the highest valued social projects
- Social impact investors face higher opportunity costs of capital than for-profit owners, since funds available for social good are relatively scarce and socially minded investors face additional portfolio constraints
- The model is consistent with the design of social impact bonds, such as the 2014 Massachusetts $12 million social impact bond aimed at reducing recidivism among at-risk young men in Boston
