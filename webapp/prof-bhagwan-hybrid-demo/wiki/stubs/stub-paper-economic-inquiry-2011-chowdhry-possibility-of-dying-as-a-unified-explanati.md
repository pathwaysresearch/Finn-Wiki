# Paper Economic Inquiry 2011 Chowdhry Possibility Of Dying As A Unified Explanation Of Why We Discount The Future Get

**Type**: RAG stub — full content in vector index, not in wiki

## Abstract

Bhagwan Chowdhry presents a parsimonious evolutionary model demonstrating that the possibility of dying from external factors (extrinsic mortality) can simultaneously explain three fundamental phenomena: (a) why organisms discount the future, (b) why organisms get weaker with age (senescence), and (c) why organisms display risk-aversion. The model builds on Williams (1957) and uses a continuous-time optimization framework where organisms allocate an endowed stock of resources over their lifetime. The paper proposes testable restrictions across species, time, and genders linking time preference, aging, and risk-aversion within a unified framework.

## Key Claims

- Extrinsic mortality (the possibility of dying from external factors) provides a single unified explanation for time discounting, aging/senescence, and risk-aversion
- Organisms disproportionately allocate resources to youth at the expense of old age because survival to old age is preconditioned on first surviving when young, but not vice versa (following Williams 1957)
- Risk-aversion emerges naturally: organisms prefer a sure endowment of resources over an uncertain endowment of equal expected value because a decrease in resources has asymmetric consequences given mortality risk
- The model uses a linear production function (f_t = x_t) to ensure that risk-aversion is not artificially induced by concavity in the production function
- Higher resource expenditure simultaneously increases offspring production and lowers the probability of death (increases survival probability), with the key assumption δ'(x_t) < 0
