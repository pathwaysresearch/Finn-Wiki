# Equivalence of Certainty Equivalent and Risk-Adjusted Discount Rate Approaches

The certainty equivalent approach and the risk-adjusted discount rate approach are two methods for adjusting for risk in discounted cash flow valuation. When both are derived from the same underlying risk model (e.g., using the same risk and return models to estimate risk premiums or certainty equivalents), they produce identical valuations. This equivalence arises because both methods are fundamentally adjusting for the same risk in a consistent manner. The choice between them is therefore not about accuracy but about transparency and simplicity: the certainty equivalent method explicitly adjusts cash flows, while the risk-adjusted discount rate method adjusts the discount rate. Depending on the context, one may be more intuitive or easier to communicate than the other.
