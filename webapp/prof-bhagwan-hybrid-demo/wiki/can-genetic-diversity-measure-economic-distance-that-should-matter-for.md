# Genetic Diversity as Economic Distance Proxy

The answer articulates a novel five-link mechanism chain connecting genetic distance to home bias: genetic distance → cultural divergence → institutional divergence → elevated perceived transaction/enforcement costs → reduced bilateral financial flows → observed home bias.

This chain is more explicit and complete than what the Wiki currently captures under the Gravity Model entry, and it positions genetic distance as an exogenous instrument for cultural-informational distance — a methodological insight that would strengthen the Wiki's treatment of economic distance proxies in home bias research.

Additionally, the inference that diaspora networks and educational exchanges can reduce effective cultural-genetic distance over time — even if biological genetic distance is fixed — is a policy-relevant synthesis not currently present in the Wiki.
