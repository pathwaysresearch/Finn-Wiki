# Prof. Bhagwan Chowdhry

**Professor of Finance** at the Indian School of Business (ISB) and Research Professor at UCLA Anderson School of Management. A **mechanism designer disguised as a finance professor** -- he sees human problems (poverty, corruption, bureaucratic decay) as incentive-alignment failures, not moral failures, and applies the tools of corporate finance to serve the world's poorest billions.

---

## Identity

- **B.Tech.** in Mechanical Engineering, Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kanpur, 1981
- **MBA** in Finance, University of Iowa, 1983; **MBA** in Economics + **Ph.D.**, University of Chicago Booth School of Business, 1989
- PhD advisor: [[mentor-network-and-influences|Merton Miller]] (Nobel laureate, co-architect of Modigliani-Miller theorem)
- **Born**: 1959 (turned 66 in 2025)

The arc: **engineer --> financial theorist --> applied inclusion advocate.** From pricing currency swaps at Chicago to redesigning the global financial architecture for 3 billion unbanked people. The tools remain constant; the ambition grows.

---

## Intellectual DNA

Two commitments, inherited from Chicago but tempered by moral urgency:

1. **Markets and incentives work** -- properly designed systems make it rational for self-interested actors to serve social good. "People respond to incentives. All else is commentary." (Steven Landsburg, Chowdhry's most-cited axiom)

2. **But markets must be designed to include everyone** -- left alone, financial systems exclude the poorest. Technology enables but does not save. Institutions must be deliberately engineered for inclusion.

The tension between these two commitments is **the engine of his entire body of work.** He is neither a naive free-marketeer nor a central planner. He is a designer of systems -- financial instruments, institutional rules, technology standards -- that channel human self-interest toward human dignity.

Core belief: *"One of my greatest joys comes from working with smart people who strive to make this world a better place, especially for people with limited resources and opportunities. I am constantly thinking about, doing research on, and getting involved in projects that will make a difference in the world."*

## How He Thinks

**[[thinking-patterns-and-frameworks]]** -- The reusable intellectual tools:
- The **Incentive-First Lens**: every proposal starts with "What incentives does this create?"
- **Equity-Over-Debt**: flexible risk-sharing beats rigid obligations, whether for sovereign nations, microentrepreneurs, or social projects
- **Mechanism Design**: design rules so self-interested agents produce good outcomes (from PhD work on security design to FAB accounts)
- **SHUb Design Standard**: Simple, Human, Ubiquitous -- the test every solution must pass
- **Concrete Arithmetic**: anchor every proposal in specific, verifiable numbers
- **Markets-Over-Mandates**: prefer market mechanisms, but design the institutions that make markets work

**[[intellectual-evolution]]** -- Three phases, one constant thread:
- **Phase 1 (1988-2005)**: Pure financial theory -- information aggregation, security design, currency swaps, sovereign defaults
- **Phase 2 (2005-2012)**: The bridge -- franchising microfinance, FAB conception, applying corporate finance to bottom-of-pyramid
- **Phase 3 (2013-present)**: Applied inclusion -- FinTech for Billions, DIRI/Aadhaar, sovereign equity, land auctions, AI in education
- **The constant thread**: Mechanism design. The application domain changes; the toolkit does not.

## What He Builds

**[[financial-inclusion-architecture]]** -- A three-layer stack for universal financial citizenship:
- **Layer 1: Identity** -- Aadhaar/DIRI. Biometric ID for <$1/person. The foundation.
- **Layer 2: Access** -- Financial Access at Birth (FAB). $100 per child, incentive-aligned.
- **Layer 3: Usability** -- SHUb framework. "Does Aadhaar + Jan Dhan + phone = included?" Answer: No. Design matters.

**[[mechanism-design-applied]]** -- The same toolkit across other domains:
- **Impact investing**: Social impact guarantees (SIGs) that align profit and social good
- **Sovereign finance**: Equity-like instruments for nations (Greece/Uber analogy)
- **Institutional reform**: IAS golden parachutes, university governance, land auctions
- **Technology**: Big data to reduce discrimination, digital currency design
