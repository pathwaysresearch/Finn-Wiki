## Abstract

Aswath Damodaran (2005) surveys the theory and evidence on valuation approaches in finance. The paper covers discounted cash flow (DCF) valuation models — from dividend discount models to excess return models — relative valuation using multiples and comparables, and the estimation challenges posed by globalisation and multi-country risk exposure. Valuation is positioned as the heart of finance: corporate investment decisions, portfolio management, and market efficiency all depend on understanding what determines the value of a firm.

## Key Claims

- There are four fundamental approaches to valuation: discounted cash flow, liquidation/accounting, relative valuation (multiples), and contingent claim (real options)
- DCF valuation has the strongest theoretical credentials but requires estimating expected cash flows and discount rates — both subject to significant uncertainty
- Relative valuation using multiples (P/E, EV/EBITDA, Price/Book) is more widely used in practice than DCF, despite weaker theoretical grounding
- The choice between equity valuation and firm valuation matters — errors from mixing equity and firm cash flows with mismatched discount rates are common
- Estimation challenges intensify as companies globalise and face risk across multiple countries
