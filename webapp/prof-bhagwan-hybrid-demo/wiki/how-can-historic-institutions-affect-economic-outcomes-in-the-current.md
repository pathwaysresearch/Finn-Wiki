# Historical Institutions and Financial Exclusion

The question of historical institutional persistence is not directly addressed in the existing Wiki or RAG sources, which focus primarily on FAB, FinTech, and financial inclusion mechanisms. However, a meaningful synthesis emerges: the financial exclusion that FAB is designed to solve is not merely a technology or poverty gap — it is in significant part an *institutionally inherited* exclusion rooted in colonial banking architectures.

The gravity model framework used in currency transaction cost analysis can be reframed as measuring institutional distance between financial systems — a novel connection between the existing Wiki content and institutional economics literature.

This connection between colonial institutional legacy, measured transaction costs in gravity models, and the architectural logic of FAB as an institutional bypass deserves explicit treatment in the Wiki.
