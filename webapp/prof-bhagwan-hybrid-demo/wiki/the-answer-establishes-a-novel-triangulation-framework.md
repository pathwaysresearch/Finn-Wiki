# Triangulation Framework for Transaction Costs

The answer establishes a novel triangulation framework: gravity model residuals and arbitrage deviations are two independent proxies for the same underlying transactions costs, and used together they bracket the distribution of costs across market participants — sophisticated arbitrageurs at the lower bound, retail and unbanked users at the upper bound.

This bracketing methodology is not explicitly stated in the Wiki and would be a meaningful addition.

Additionally, the connection between persistent CIP deviations in emerging market currency pairs and remittance corridor welfare costs for the poor is a synthesis that bridges the Gravity Model wiki page with the Financial Inclusion Architecture wiki page in a way not currently captured.
