# Gravity Model Residuals and Remittance Corridors

The Wiki entry on the Gravity Model focuses on the theoretical framing and its application to transactions costs via residual analysis.

A novel connection worth capturing: the gravity residual in currency markets maps almost directly onto high-cost remittance corridors in developing nations, making it not just a measurement tool but a welfare diagnostic.

Additionally, the 'threshold behavior' insight — that below a critical volume of bilateral transactions, market makers won't invest in friction-reducing infrastructure — connects the gravity model to the Financial Inclusion Architecture's FinTech for Billions layer.

This cross-wiki linkage between the gravity model and financial inclusion outcomes is not currently explicit in either Wiki page and would strengthen both.
