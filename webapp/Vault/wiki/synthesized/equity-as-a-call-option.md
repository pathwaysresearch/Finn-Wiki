# Equity as a Call Option

# Equity as a Call Option

The concept that a corporation's equity is effectively a call option on the firm's assets, with a strike price equal to the face value of its debt. This perspective arises from limited liability: equity holders are residual claimants who receive everything after debt is paid, which gives them upside potential like a call option, while their downside is limited to zero—mirroring the payoff of a long call.

## Relationships

- Builds on option pricing theory to analyze capital structure.
- Provides foundation for understanding debt as a short put position.
- Enables techniques such as capital structure arbitrage.

## Implications

By viewing equity as a call option, one can see how changes in asset volatility affect equity value, why leverage increases equity risk, and how financial distress alters option-like payoffs.
